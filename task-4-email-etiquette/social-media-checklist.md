# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Name:** Aadi Jain | **Reg No:** 25BCE10331 | CSE, First Year | VIT Bhopal University

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and write a bio that reflects your academic interests and goals — recruiters and professors do look at these.

2. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parents, or a future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings.** Review and update the privacy settings on all your accounts. Limit who can see your personal information, location, and posts.

4. **Give credit when sharing content.** If you share someone else's article, photo, or idea, tag them or mention the source. This is basic digital ethics.

5. **Engage positively and constructively.** Support peers, share useful academic content, and participate in professional discussions that add value to your field.

6. **Use LinkedIn for professional networking.** Connect with seniors, professors, and industry professionals through LinkedIn rather than on personal social media platforms.

7. **Log out of shared devices.** Always log out of your social media accounts when using a shared computer, lab system, or a friend's device.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't post complaints about professors, classmates, or your institution publicly.** Even a vague post can be screenshot and identified. Raise concerns through the proper channels instead.

2. **Don't share personal information like your address, phone number, or daily schedule** on public profiles. This creates safety risks and invites unwanted contact.

3. **Don't engage in or tolerate cyberbullying.** Do not post, share, or like content that mocks, harasses, or humiliates another person. Report it instead.

4. **Don't overshare location data.** Avoid tagging your real-time location in posts, especially when you are alone or away from campus.

5. **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers** without verifying who they are. Fake profiles are commonly used for phishing and social engineering attacks.

6. **Don't post during exams or when you have claimed to be unwell.** Inconsistencies between what you post and what you tell your institution can lead to serious consequences.

7. **Don't spread unverified news or rumours.** Always verify information from a reliable source before sharing. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — harms others and your own credibility.
