# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used

**Canva** (canva.com) – free, browser-based graphic design platform.

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled **"Your Guide to Digital Literacy"** and is designed as a single-page visual resource for first-year B.Tech students. It covers three key topics:

1. **What Digital Literacy Is** – A short definition explaining that digital literacy means the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technology responsibly and effectively.  
     
2. **Safe Internet Practices** – Tips including using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal data online.  
     
3. **Professional Online Presence** – A brief section on why platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn matter for students, and how to keep profiles clean and professional from day one.

The design uses a clean two-column layout with colour-coded sections, icons, and concise bullet points so that the information is easy to scan at a glance. The colour palette is blue and white to keep the tone professional.

## Reflection

I used Canva's free template library as a starting point, which made it much easier to get going without prior design experience. The most interesting part was learning that visual hierarchy — how you arrange headings, icons, and text — has a huge impact on how readable a design is. The most difficult part was deciding how much information to include. My first draft was too text-heavy, so I had to cut content and trust the icons to carry meaning. This taught me that effective digital communication is as much about what you leave out as what you put in.
