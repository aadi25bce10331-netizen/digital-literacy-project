## Case Study: The University Portal Phishing Attack

### What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker impersonates a trusted organisation or individual — such as a bank, university, or government body — to trick a victim into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or financial details. The name derives from the idea of "fishing" for victims using deceptive bait.

---

### How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

**Step 1 – Target Selection:** The attacker identifies a group of potential victims. In this scenario, first-year university students are targeted during exam registration season, when they are anxious and checking their email frequently.

**Step 2 – Crafting the Bait:** The attacker creates a fake email that appears to come from the university's official IT helpdesk. The email mimics the university's logo, colour scheme, and email signature. The subject line reads: *"Urgent: Your exam registration will be cancelled — verify your account within 24 hours."*

**Step 3 – Delivery:** The phishing email is sent to hundreds of student email addresses obtained from a public student forum or leaked database.

**Step 4 – The Hook:** The email contains a link to a fake university login page that looks identical to the real portal. The URL is slightly different — for example, `vitbhopal-portal-login.net` instead of `vtop.vitbhopal.ac.in`.

**Step 5 – Data Capture:** When a student enters their username and password on the fake page, the attacker silently records the credentials and redirects the student to the real portal to avoid suspicion.

**Step 6 – Exploitation:** The attacker uses the stolen credentials to access the student's academic account, change contact details, view internal academic records, or sell the credentials on the dark web.

---

### Who Is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks in academic settings typically target first-year students who are unfamiliar with official communication channels, students under deadline pressure (registration, fee payment, exam schedules), and anyone who has recently joined a new platform or portal.

---

### Consequences

For the victim, the consequences include loss of access to academic accounts, potential academic fraud (grade manipulation, identity misuse), financial loss if bank details are linked, and significant emotional distress. For the institution, phishing attacks can lead to reputational damage and large-scale data breaches.
