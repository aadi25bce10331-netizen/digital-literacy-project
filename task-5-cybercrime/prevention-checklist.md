## General Online Safety

1. **Verify the sender's email address before clicking any link.**
   Look at the full email address, not just the display name. Official university emails will come from `@vitbhopal.ac.in`, not variations like `vitbhopal-helpdesk@gmail.com`.

2. **Never enter your password on a page you reached by clicking an email link.**
   Always navigate to official portals directly by typing the URL in your browser. Bookmark important URLs like VTOP, your bank, and your university portal.

3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   This includes your college email, Gmail, and any platform linked to your academic or personal data. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA prevents unauthorised access.

4. **Use a unique, strong password for every important account.**
   A strong password has at least 12 characters and includes uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple sites. Consider using a password manager.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated.**
   Software updates often contain security patches that fix vulnerabilities attackers exploit. Enable automatic updates wherever possible.

6. **Do not download attachments from unknown senders.**
   Attachments in phishing emails often contain malware or ransomware. If you are unsure about an email, contact the sender through a different channel to verify.


## Financial & UPI Safety (India-specific)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone — not even a bank representative.**
   Legitimate banks and payment platforms will NEVER ask for your PIN or OTP. If someone calls requesting this information, hang up immediately.

8. **Be cautious of "receive money" requests on UPI apps.**
   A common scam involves sending a payment request and claiming it is a money transfer. Entering your PIN to "accept" money actually authorises a payment FROM your account. Always read the transaction screen carefully before entering your PIN.



## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know has been a victim of phishing, online fraud, or any cybercrime:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24×7, all states)
- Report immediately — early reporting increases the chance of recovering lost funds and catching attackers.
